The northern lights
What are the chances of seeing the northern lights?+
Our aurora success rate is 97.54% verified over the last 3 years. This is one of the highest in Tromsø. We achieve this by following real-time aurora forecasts, chasing clear skies across the region — and if needed, crossing into Finland. The main enemy is clouds, not aurora activity. Tromsø has many microclimates, so clear sky is almost always findable within a 1–3 hour drive.
When is the best time to see the northern lights?+
The northern lights season in Tromsø runs from late September to early April. You need darkness to see them, which means they're not visible in summer. Peak season is October–February, when nights are longest and aurora activity tends to be highest. We recommend staying at least 4–5 nights to maximise your chances.
What if it's cloudy?+
Clouds are the main challenge — not aurora activity. This is exactly why you need a guide. We monitor multiple weather forecasts in real time and know every microclimate in the region. When it's overcast in Tromsø, it's often clear 30–60 minutes away. We drive until we find clear sky, even if that means crossing into Finland. Our 97.54% success rate is proof this works.
Can I see the northern lights from the city?+
On very active nights with strong displays, yes — but light pollution significantly reduces visibility. To see the subtle, dancing, multi-coloured aurora, you need to get away from city lights. Our tours take you to dark, remote locations where the full display is visible even on moderate nights.

We believe in total honesty — if conditions look very poor, we will tell you upfront and give you the option to reschedule before departure.
What to wear & bring
What clothing do you provide?+
We provide a full Arctic thermal suit (onesie-style outer layer) for all guests on both tours. This keeps you warm in temperatures down to –20°C. We do not provide boots or footwear — please bring your own warm, waterproof shoes or boots. Wool socks are strongly recommended.
What should I wear underneath the thermal suit?+
Layer up! We recommend: wool or thermal base layer (top and bottom), a mid-layer fleece or down jacket, and warm socks. The thermal suit goes over all of this. Even if it feels warm inside, temperatures at night and in open landscapes can drop significantly — it's always better to be too warm than too cold.
Can I take photos of the northern lights?+
Yes! Your guide takes free professional aurora photos that are sent to you the morning after via email or WeTransfer. We also bring tripods for guests who want to take their own shots. For smartphone users: use night mode, turn off the flash, and use the timer to avoid camera shake. Modern iPhones and Samsung Galaxy phones produce excellent aurora shots.
